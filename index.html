Research Interests

My research interests lie in the field of nanoscience/nanomaterials/nanotechnology and its applications in various avenues, including, but not limited to, bioengineering. I have previously worked on a project under Dr. Anirban Roy of BITS Pilani to understand the feasibility and benefits of using piezoelectric material in gym equipment to generate electricity from the work done on them. I have also worked on a semester long study oriented project under Dr. Richa Singhal of BITS Pilani to learn about the use of electrospun nanofibers in sensors used in wearable technology. The project work involved extensive literature review and helped me learn about the various nanofiber fabrication techniques. I also explored various piezoelectric material based sensors, harvesters and actuators; their applications and recent advancements in shoe-embedded technologies, HMIs and electronic skin. As a part of a compulsory degree requirement, I have interned at Atomic Energy Regulatory Board, Mumbai as a summer research intern. As an intern, I was given an opportunity to explore the working of nuclear power plants in India. I also developed the thermophysical property module for the Containment Code (a proprietary software to simulate the containment chambers of the nuclear power plants in India). I am currently working on a semester long lab-oriented project under the guidance of Dr. Jegatha Nambi Krishnan of BITS Pilani to explore the field of polymer blend membranes, with special emphasis on BMNCs. I am also working on a reading course under Dr. Paramita Haldar of BITS Pilani to learn about molecular dynamics simulations. The details of all my internships, course work and projects can be found in my resume.

Simulation Code for a Green Gym

I studied the feasibility of having a "green gym" and developed a code to simulate it using Java. A green gym is a model gym that does not require any external input of energy and instead, generates electricity itself. The equipment used are based on piezoelectric material, capable of generating electricity from the mechanical work done on them. The project was done under the guidance of Dr. Anirban Roy of BITS Pilani (Goa campus), India.
